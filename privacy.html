MailDesk Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 25, 2026

MailDesk is a Windows desktop mail client focused on Gmail workflows. This policy explains what data MailDesk accesses, how that data is used, and where it is stored.

Data MailDesk Accesses

MailDesk accesses Gmail data only after you sign in with a Google account and grant permission. Depending on the permissions you approve, MailDesk may access your Gmail messages, labels, message metadata, attachments, and account profile information needed to provide mail client features.

How Data Is Used

MailDesk uses Gmail data to display mail, search messages, manage labels, send and reply to mail, download attachments, synchronize inbox state, and show user-configured Windows notifications. MailDesk may detect verification codes in message subjects and snippets so it can show verification-code notifications when you enable that feature.

Local Storage

MailDesk stores OAuth tokens locally on your Windows device using secure storage. Mail cache, settings, logs, and notification state are also stored locally on your device to operate the app and improve reliability.

Data Sharing

MailDesk does not sell user data, does not use Gmail data for advertising, and does not transfer Gmail data to third parties except as required to provide the user-requested Gmail functionality through Google APIs.

Deleting Data

You can remove your account from MailDesk to delete locally stored authentication tokens for that account. You may also uninstall MailDesk and remove the app's local data from Windows if you no longer want the app to store local cache, settings, or logs.

Contact

For privacy questions, contact the MailDesk publisher through the support contact listed in the Microsoft Store.
